Personal Projects

Archived

  • BSc (Hons) Final Year Project
    A software capable of manipulating and benchmarking arbitrary reflectance models, implemented in C++/Qt/OpenGL. I developed this software for my dissertation, in which I evaluated the suitability of a diffraction-based reflectance model for real-time rendering solutions. This involved a performance comparison to Unreal Engine's shading model and a user study measuring the practicality of the diffraction-based model with non-experts.
  • A CG Boxing Match
    A 'computer graphics' coursework submitted in the third year of my BSc (Hons) degree, demonstrating different features of animation and shading. This was written in C++/Qt/OpenGL.
  • Identifying Distinguishing Features of African English Dialects (Paper)
    A 'data mining' coursework submitted in the second year of my BSc (Hons) degree. The paper details collecting, cleaning and classifying a corpus of African-Engish dialects to identify distinguishing features of the Ghanaian-English dialect. This coursework involved working in a team of five to collect and clean multiple sub-corpora using Sketch Engine. Classification was then done independently in Weka, a machine learning tool for data preparation, visualisation and classification.
  • Procedural Low-poly Terrain Generation
    Infinite terrain using simplex noise to generate height data, implemented in C++/OpenGL.
  • The Mathematics of Frets (Paper)
    A 'mathematics of music' coursework submitted in the first year of my BSc (Hons) degree, discussing the mathematical origin of frets on the modern guitar.
